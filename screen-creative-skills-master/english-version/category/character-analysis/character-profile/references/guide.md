# Character Biography Generation Guide

## I. Core Definition

### Important Character
"Important Character" is a dramatic concept, also known as "protagonist" or "important role," key figures who drive story development and plot progression.

Their behavior, decisions, and interactions with other characters usually form the core part of the story. Main characters are not always positive heroic figures; they may be villains, or even supporting characters with complex personalities. But regardless, they occupy important positions in the story and have a direct impact on the story's outcome.

## II. Analysis Dimensions

### 1. Basic Information
- **Name**: Full name or common appellation of the character.
- **Identity**: Occupation, social status, family role, etc.
- **Appearance**: Physical features, dress, temperament and demeanor.

### 2. Personality Characteristics
- **Main Personality**: Core personality keywords (such as brave, cowardly, shrewd, honest).
- **Behavioral Patterns**: Habitual reactions under pressure or conflict.
- **Language Style**: Way of speaking, catchphrases, speech speed and tone.

### 3. Background Story
- **Growth Experience**: Key past events that shaped current personality.
- **Family Background**: Influence of original family.
- **Important Experiences**: Key turning points before the story began.

### 4. Relationships and Functions
- **Character Relationships**: Interaction patterns with other main characters.
- **Story Function**: Role assumed in the plot (such as obstacle, helper, mentor).
- **Goals and Dilemmas**: What the character wants (desire) and what obstacles they face (conflict).

## III. Generation Specifications

1. **Quantity Requirement**: Must identify all main characters and important supporting characters in the story text, usually not less than 8 (if the story has enough characters).
2. **Word Count Control**: Each character biography strictly controlled between 300-500 words.
3. **Content Source**: Strictly based on story text analysis, no creation out of thin air.
4. **Narrative Perspective**: Even if the original text is first-person, biographies should use third-person objective description.

## IV. Workflow

1. **Deep Reading**: Read through the full text, mark all appearing characters and their behaviors.
2. **Character Screening**: Identify "important characters" who drive plot development.
3. **Information Integration**: Piece together fragmented character information scattered in the text (appearance, dialogue, behavior) into a complete picture.
4. **Biography Writing**: Write fluent character biographies according to the structured format, ensuring inclusion of core elements such as personality, background, relationships, and goals.
