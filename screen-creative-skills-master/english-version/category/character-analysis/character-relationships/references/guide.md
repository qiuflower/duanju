# Character Relationship Analysis Guide

## I. Core Definition

### Character Relationship
Character relationship refers to the interactions, connections, and mutual influences between various characters in a story or script.

These relationships encompass everything, including:
- **Family Relationships**: Parents, children, siblings, spouses, etc.
- **Social Relationships**: Friends, classmates, colleagues, neighbors, etc.
- **Emotional Relationships**: Lovers, crushes, exes, love rivals, etc.
- **Functional Relationships**: Mentor-disciple, master-servant, allies, enemies, competitors, etc.

Character relationships play a crucial role in story development, usually helping to shape story conflicts and resolutions, and driving story progress. By analyzing character relationships, you can more deeply understand the story's themes and meanings.

## II. Analysis Dimensions

### 1. Relationship Type
Clearly define the relationship attributes between characters.
- **Strong Relationships**: Blood, marriage, life-and-death friendships.
- **Weak Relationships**: Nodding acquaintances, temporary cooperation.
- **Positive Relationships**: Mutual assistance, liking, support.
- **Negative Relationships**: Hatred, jealousy, obstruction.

### 2. Relationship Characteristics
Describe the unique aspects of that relationship. For example:
- A "love-hate" brotherly relationship.
- A "both mentor and friend" supervisor-subordinate relationship.
- A "seemingly harmonious but actually undercurrent surging" spousal relationship.

### 3. Development Process
Analyze the trajectory of relationship changes over time:
- **Establishment**: How did the relationship begin?
- **Development**: What key events were experienced to deepen or distance?
- **Turning Point**: Were there major changes causing the relationship nature to change?
- **Outcome**: What state is the relationship in at the end of the story?

### 4. Relationship Impact
Analyze the role of that relationship in the story:
- How does it drive plot development?
- How does it shape character personalities?
- How does it express story themes?

## III. Generation Specifications

1. **Quantity Requirement**: Must analyze no fewer than 12 pairs of character relationships (if story volume allows), ensuring coverage of main and secondary character networks.
2. **Content Accuracy**: Strictly summarize based on story text, do not make subjective conjectures.
3. **Comprehensive and Detailed**: Not only qualify relationships, but also analyze interaction details and reasons for changes.

## IV. Workflow

1. **Character Identification**: List all key characters in the story.
2. **Relationship Connection**: Build character relationship matrix, identify connections between each pair.
3. **Deep Analysis**: Conduct "qualitative" (type) and "quantitative" (interaction depth) analysis of each pair of relationships.
4. **Summary Writing**: Write detailed relationship introductions following the [Character A-Character B] format.
