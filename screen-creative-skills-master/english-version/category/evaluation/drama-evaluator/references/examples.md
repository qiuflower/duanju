# Vertical Screen Drama Evaluation Expert - Detailed Examples

## Example: Evaluating Urban Counterattack Story

### Input

```
Please evaluate the potential of the following story adapted as a vertical screen drama:

Story Synopsis:
Lin Qian is a workplace woman working at an advertising company. Her supervisor, Manager Wang, is a snob who often humiliates her in public. One day, an important client visits the company. Manager Wang wants Lin Qian to drink with the client. After she refuses, she suffers even harsher humiliation.

Lin Qian doesn't give up. She works hard and wins an important project for the company with her ability and creativity. At the project launch, Lin Qian speaks as the project leader, and Manager Wang has to acknowledge her ability. Finally, Lin Qian receives recognition and promotion from the company.
```

### Output

```
[Vertical Screen Drama Adaptation Potential Evaluation Report]

[Core Satisfaction Point]: Score: 7.8
Core satisfaction point: Bottom-tier workplace woman uses intelligence and effort to slap snobbish supervisor, ultimately succeeds in counterattacking

Analysis and Evaluation: This satisfaction point has a certain degree of universality. Many workplace people have experienced similar suppression and desire for recognition. But the satisfaction point presentation is relatively mild, lacking extreme dramatic conflict.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Close to reality, easy to resonate
- Weaknesses: Satisfaction not strong enough, lacks the pleasure of "slapping"

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Add extreme scenes of protagonist humiliation to build power for subsequent slapping
2. Make protagonist's counterattack more rapid and direct, reduce setup
3. Enhance shock of identity reversal (e.g., protagonist is actually a hidden big shot)

[Story Type]: Score: 7.5
Story type: Urban workplace counterattack + female growth

Analysis and Evaluation: Uses classic counterattack pattern, but insufficient innovation. Relatively traditional tropes, lacking unique memorable points.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Clear genre, clear target audience
- Weaknesses: Lacks innovative elements, serious homogenization

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Add unique setup elements (e.g., protagonist has special abilities, special identity, etc.)
2. Extremize classic tropes, achieve the extreme in similar themes
3. Integrate hot elements (e.g., book transmigration, rebirth, system, etc.)

[Character Setting]: Score: 7.3
Main characters:
- Protagonist Lin Qian: Workplace woman, resilient and independent
- Antagonist Manager Wang: Snobbish supervisor, caricatured villain

Analysis and Evaluation: Protagonist persona relatively flat, lacks extreme contrast and tagged characteristics. Villain too caricatured, lacks layers.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Clear character personalities
- Weaknesses: Lacks extremization, weak memorable points

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Make protagonist persona more extreme (e.g., super social phobic but must socialize, rookie on surface big shot underneath, etc.)
2. Add protagonist's unique skills or traits
3. Make villain more arrogant, enhance slapping satisfaction

[Character Relationships]: Score: 7.2
Main character relationships: Lin Qian ↔ Manager Wang (opposition), Lin Qian ↔ Colleagues (support)

Analysis and Evaluation: Character relationships clear but tension insufficient, lacking extreme opposition and tight bonding.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Relationship structure simple and clear
- Weaknesses: Lacks dramatic tension and conflict intensity

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Enhance direct opposition and conflict between protagonist and villain
2. Increase complexity and layers of character relationships
3. Make protagonist-antagonist confrontation more direct and intense

[Plot Bridges]: Score: 7.0
Plot bridges: Protagonist humiliated by supervisor → Protagonist works hard → Protagonist gets opportunity to prove herself → Protagonist successfully slaps supervisor

Analysis and Evaluation: Plot development relatively flat, lacking extreme emotional ups and downs and dramatic conflict. Rhythm not tight enough, satisfaction release not strong enough.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Plot development complete and reasonable
- Weaknesses: Lacks explosive points, insufficient suspense setup

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Skip process, cut directly to core conflict
2. Set strong suspense hooks at end of each episode
3. Increase drama and visual impact of plot
4. Make slapping scenes more shocking and direct

[Comprehensive Evaluation]:
This story has certain adaptation potential, overall quality above average, but lacks the extremity and dramatic nature needed for a hit. Needs significant optimization in core satisfaction points, character settings, plot bridges, etc., to meet hit vertical screen drama standards.

Core risk points: Serious homogenization, lacking unique memorable points, insufficient satisfaction
Core opportunity points: Close to reality, clear audience base, easy to resonate

Recommendation: Proceed with caution, recommend significant optimization before considering development
```

## Example: Evaluating Sweet Romance Story

### Input

```
Please evaluate the adaptation potential of the following story:

Story Synopsis:
Rich second-generation Gu Yan hides his identity to experience life, becoming a food delivery rider. He meets Su Ya who is being humiliated by her ex-boyfriend and helps her out. Su Ya thinks Gu Yan is just an ordinary delivery rider, and they gradually develop feelings.

The ex-boyfriend appears with his new girlfriend, again humiliating Su Ya and Gu Yan. At the critical moment, Gu Yan's identity is revealed - he is the chairman of Gu Group. The ex-boyfriend and girlfriend are kicked out of the restaurant. Gu Yan and Su Ya end up together.
```

### Output

```
[Vertical Screen Drama Adaptation Potential Evaluation Report]

[Core Satisfaction Point]: Score: 8.2
Core satisfaction point: Hidden big-shot CEO reveals identity at critical moment, ruthlessly slaps snobbish ex-boyfriend

Analysis and Evaluation: This satisfaction point has extremely strong universality. "Identity reversal" is one of the most classic and effective satisfaction points in vertical screen dramas. The satisfaction from identity contrast is strong and direct.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Classic satisfaction point, broad audience base, strong satisfaction
- Weaknesses: Relatively common trope, needs innovation in details

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Enhance extremity of identity contrast (e.g., make ex-boyfriend's humiliation more arrogant)
2. Make identity revelation method more dramatized and visualized
3. Increase sweetness during setup phase, strengthen audience anticipation

[Story Type]: Score: 8.0
Story type: Sweet romance + slapping + identity contrast

Analysis and Evaluation: Combines three hot elements: sweet romance, slapping, identity contrast. Precise genre positioning. Although classic pattern, can become a hit with proper execution.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Classic element combination, clear target audience
- Weaknesses: Needs to avoid homogenization, create differentiation

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Innovate in sweet romance bridges, avoid becoming formulaic
2. Strengthen drama of identity contrast
3. Add unique memorable points (e.g., special meeting method, unique interaction pattern)

[Character Setting]: Score: 7.8
Main characters:
- Protagonist Gu Yan: Hidden identity rich second-generation CEO, ordinary appearance big shot underneath
- Protagonist Su Ya: Humiliated ordinary woman, innocent and kind
- Antagonist ex-boyfriend: Snob, caricatured villain

Analysis and Evaluation: Protagonist persona has contrast sense, but can be more extreme. Ex-boyfriend persona too caricatured, can add layers.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Clear character relationships, has contrast sense
- Weaknesses: Persona can be more extremized and tagged

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Make Gu Yan's "hidden identity" more extreme (e.g., super stingy, super frugal)
2. Give Su Ya more distinct tags (e.g., foodie, social phobic, etc.)
3. Make ex-boyfriend's arrogance more extreme, build power for slapping

[Character Relationships]: Score: 7.5
Main character relationships: Gu Yan ↔ Su Ya (sweet romance), Gu Yan ↔ Ex-boyfriend (opposition), Su Ya ↔ Ex-boyfriend (ex relationship)

Analysis and Evaluation: Character relationships clear, has sweet romance line and opposition line, complete structure. But relationship extremity and drama can be enhanced.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Complete relationship structure, multiple threads
- Weaknesses: Extremity and dramatic tension can be enhanced

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Enhance sweetness between Gu Yan and Su Ya
2. Enhance opposition intensity between ex-boyfriend and protagonists
3. Can add a subplot (e.g., pressure from Gu Yan's family)

[Plot Bridges]: Score: 7.8
Plot bridges: Su Ya humiliated by ex-boyfriend → Gu Yan helps out → They develop feelings → Ex-boyfriend appears again → Gu Yan's identity revealed → Successful slap

Analysis and Evaluation: Complete plot structure, has beginning, development, twist and resolution. But plot extremity and suspense setup can be optimized.

Benchmarking market hits:
- Strengths: Complete plot, tight rhythm
- Weaknesses: Insufficient suspense setup, emotional extremity can be enhanced

Optimization Suggestions:
1. Set strong suspense hooks at end of each episode
2. Make identity revelation method more dramatized and visualized
3. Increase sweetness of sweet romance bridges

[Comprehensive Evaluation]:
This story has strong adaptation potential, precise genre positioning, clear satisfaction points. After optimization in character settings, plot bridges, etc., has potential to become a hit. Overall quality above average, recommend development.

Core risk points: Homogenization risk, needs differentiation in details
Core opportunity points: Classic satisfaction point, broad audience base, easy to become hit

Recommendation: Recommend development with simultaneous optimization
```
