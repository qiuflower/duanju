# Vertical Screen Drama Evaluation Standards

## Score Level Description

| Level | Score Range | Rating | Development Recommendation |
|-------|-------------|--------|---------------------------|
| S | 8.5+ | Excellent | Strongly recommend to proceed, has extremely strong competitiveness and development value |
| A | 8.0-8.4 | Good | Recommend to proceed, has strong competitiveness, needs some modifications |
| B | 7.5-7.9 | Qualified | Proceed with caution, average competitiveness, needs significant modifications |
| C | 7.4 and below | Poor | Not recommended to proceed, insufficient competitiveness |

## Core Satisfaction Point Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Universality Degree (9-10 points standard)
- **Rooted in universal desires or suppressions of the general public**
  - Financial freedom, class crossing
  - True love recognized, emotions reciprocated
  - Justice served, villains punished
  - Abilities recognized, talents showcased

### 2. Clarity and Concreteness (9-10 points standard)
- **Concrete and visualizable satisfaction points**
  - Can be expressed with specific scenes and actions
  - Can be presented with exaggerated expressions and dramatic movements
  - Audience can immediately understand and resonate

### 3. Rhythmic Progression (9-10 points standard)
- **Layered progression of small, medium, and large satisfaction points**
  - A small satisfaction point every 1-2 episodes
  - A medium satisfaction point every 3-5 episodes
  - A large satisfaction point before the finale
  - Satisfaction intensity gradually increases

## Story Type Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Strong Setup (9-10 points standard)
- **Extremely attractive supernatural setup**
  - Rebirth, transmigration, system, supernatural abilities
  - Hidden identity, tycoon identity
  - Special abilities, special missions

### 2. Classic + Innovation (9-10 points standard)
- **Use classic patterns to attract audiences, integrate innovative elements**
  - Use audience-familiar genre patterns
  - Innovate in details
  - Differentiate in character settings

### 3. Extremization (9-10 points standard)
- **Extremize audience-familiar tropes**
  - Villains extremely arrogant
  - Protagonists extremely tolerant then extremely explosive
  - Slaps extremely satisfying
  - Satisfaction extremely released

## Character Setting Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Contrast Sense (9-10 points standard)
- **Protagonist image contrast extremely strong**
  - Rookie on surface, big shot underneath
  - Weak appearance, strong heart
  - Huge identity/status contrast
  - Distinct personality trait contrast

### 2. Clear Desire (9-10 points standard)
- **Protagonist desire singular and clear**
  - Revenge, slapping, counterattack, proving oneself
  - Protecting, saving, seeking truth
  - Desire贯穿始终, drives all actions

### 3. Functionalization (9-10 points standard)
- **Villains highly caricatured, functions clear**
  - Arrogant and bullying, obstructing protagonist
  - Jealous and framing, creating conflict
  - Clear functions, exist to provide slapping targets for protagonist

## Character Relationship Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Strong Conflict (9-10 points standard)
- **Character relationships extremely binary opposition**
  - Protagonist vs. Villain: life and death
  - True love vs. Hypocrisy: incompatible
  - Justice vs. Evil: irreconcilable

### 2. Strong Bonding (9-10 points standard)
- **Character relationships extremely tight**
  - Blood relations: family, enemies
  - Emotional relations: first love, ex, true love
  - Interest relations: partners, competitors

### 3. Power Structure (9-10 points standard)
- **Clear power hierarchy**
  - High class vs. low class
  - Strong vs. weak
  - Rulers vs. ruled
  - Class crossing is core satisfaction point

## Plot Bridge Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Cut to Conflict (9-10 points standard)
- **Skip process, cut directly to core conflict**
  - Grab audience in first 3 seconds
  - Show conflict directly, no background setup
  - Strong suspense setup, trigger curiosity

### 2. Emotional Extremes (9-10 points standard)
- **Each bridge serves only one extreme emotion**
  - Anger, moved, satisfied, tense
  - Single emotion extremely amplified
  - Emotional transition rapid and powerful

### 3. Visual Transformation (9-10 points standard)
- **Can transform into exaggerated expressions, dramatic movements**
  - Slaps, kneeling, kowtowing
  - Splashing water, face-slapping, tearing
  - Strong visual impact, memorable points

### 4. Suspense Setup (9-10 points standard)
- **Set strong suspense at end of each episode**
  - Identity about to be revealed
  - Truth about to be exposed
  - Fate about to change
  - Strongly attract audience to watch next episode

## Evaluation Process

1. **First Reading**: Grasp story overall, form initial impression
2. **Dimension Analysis**: Analyze and score one by one across five dimensions
3. **Benchmark Analysis**: Compare with market hits, find gaps
4. **Comprehensive Scoring**: Calculate total score, determine level
5. **Suggestion Provide**: Provide specific actionable optimization suggestions

## Evaluation Notes

- **Objective and Fair**: Evaluate based on standards, not influenced by personal preferences
- **Comprehensive and Detailed**: Analyze from multiple dimensions, ensure evaluation complete
- **Benchmark Learning**: Reference market hits, learn successful experience
- **Specific Suggestions**: Provide actionable optimization suggestions, avoid being vague
