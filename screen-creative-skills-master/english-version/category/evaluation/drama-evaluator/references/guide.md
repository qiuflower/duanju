# Vertical Screen Drama Evaluation Guide

## Evaluation Core Philosophy

Vertical screen drama evaluation is based on the special attributes of vertical screen dramas (short and concise, fast-paced, strong satisfaction), conducting professional evaluation of stories from five dimensions: core satisfaction points, story type, character settings, character relationships, and plot bridges, analyzing their potential for adaptation into hit vertical screen dramas.

---

## Evaluation Framework

### I. Core Satisfaction Point Evaluation

Core satisfaction points are the soul of vertical screen dramas and the fundamental driving force for attracting audiences to continue watching.

#### 1. Universality Degree

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether satisfaction points are rooted in universal desires or suppressions of the general public
- Whether they can evoke broad emotional resonance
- Whether they have cross-group appeal

**Excellent Standards**:
- Satisfaction points rooted in universal values (such as justice defeating evil, underdog counterattack, invincible true love)
- Can evoke strong resonance and emotional projection
- Broad appeal across groups and age layers

#### 2. Clarity and Concreteness

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether satisfaction points are concrete and visualizable
- Whether they can be quickly understood and perceived
- Whether there are clear satisfaction release points

**Excellent Standards**:
- Satisfaction points are concrete and palpable, not abstract
- Can be visually presented with images
- Satisfaction release points are clear and distinct

#### 3. Rhythmic Progression

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether there are layers of small, medium, and large satisfaction points
- Whether satisfaction points are distributed reasonably
- Whether satisfaction continuously accumulates

**Excellent Standards**:
- Clear hierarchy of satisfaction points, progressing in layers
- Even distribution of satisfaction points, tight rhythm
- Continuous accumulation of satisfaction, culminating in major release

---

### II. Story Type Evaluation

Story type evaluation determines the story's genre positioning and market competitiveness.

#### 1. Strong Setup

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether there are extremely attractive supernatural setups
- Whether the setup is novel and unique
- Whether the setup has appeal

**Excellent Standards**:
- Setup is novel and unique, eye-catching
- Setup perfectly integrated with story
- Setup itself has strong appeal

#### 2. Classic + Innovation

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether classic patterns are used to attract audiences
- Whether innovative elements are integrated
- Whether classic and innovation are balanced

**Excellent Standards**:
- Skilled use of classic patterns
- Clever integration of innovative elements
- Perfect balance between classic and innovation

#### 3. Extremization

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether audience-familiar tropes are extremized
- Whether extremization degree is sufficient
- Whether extremization is within reasonable range

**Excellent Standards**:
- Push tropes to the extreme
- Extremization degree exceeds expectations
- Extremization within acceptable range

---

### III. Character Setting Evaluation

Character setting evaluation determines the effectiveness and appeal of characters.

#### 1. Contrast Sense

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether protagonist image contrast is extreme
- Whether contrast is vivid and interesting
- Whether contrast serves satisfaction points

**Excellent Standards**:
- Extreme contrast, forming strong comparison
- Vivid and interesting contrast, memorable
- Contrast effectively serves satisfaction point release

#### 2. Clear Desire

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether protagonist desire is singular and clear
- Whether desire drives plot development
- Whether audiences can quickly understand protagonist desire

**Excellent Standards**:
- Singular and clear desire, clear at a glance
- Desire powerfully drives plot development
- Audiences can quickly understand and identify

#### 3. Functionalization

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether antagonists are highly caricatured
- Whether antagonist functions are clear
- Whether antagonists effectively create conflict

**Excellent Standards**:
- Antagonists are caricatured but memorable
- Antagonist functions are clear, effectively create conflict
- Antagonist presence strengthens satisfaction points

---

### IV. Character Relationship Evaluation

Character relationship evaluation determines the dramatic tension of character relationships.

#### 1. Strong Conflict

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether character relationships are extremely binary opposition
- Whether conflict is strong
- Whether conflict continuously escalates

**Excellent Standards**:
- Relationships extremely binary opposed, incompatible
- Strong conflict, swords drawn
- Continuous escalation of conflict, constantly intensifying

#### 2. Strong Bonding

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether character relationships are extremely tight
- Whether they cannot be easily separated
- Whether relationships drive plot development

**Excellent Standards**:
- Relationships extremely tight, difficult to separate
- Multiple bonds, complex interweaving
- Relationships powerfully drive plot development

#### 3. Power Structure

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether there is clear power hierarchy
- Whether power structure is clear
- Whether power reversal is exciting

**Excellent Standards**:
- Clear power structure, distinct strata
- Power comparison clear at a glance
- Exciting and shocking power reversal

---

### V. Plot Bridge Evaluation

Plot bridge evaluation determines the plot's satisfaction-building ability.

#### 1. Cut to Conflict

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether process is skipped to cut directly to core conflict
- Whether opening is attractive
- Whether conflict is quickly established

**Excellent Standards**:
- Skip setup, strike directly at core conflict
- Grab attention in first 3 seconds
- Quickly establish strong conflict

#### 2. Emotional Extremes

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether each bridge serves only one extreme emotion
- Whether emotion is strong
- Whether emotion switches quickly

**Excellent Standards**:
- Each bridge corresponds to single extreme emotion
- Strong emotion, fully expressed
- Quick emotional switching, no dragging

#### 3. Visual Transformation

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether it can transform into exaggerated expressions
- Whether it can transform into dramatic movements
- Whether it has visual impact

**Excellent Standards**:
- Exaggerated expressions, strengthen emotions
- Dramatic movements, enhance conflict
- Strong visual impact, shocks audience

#### 4. Suspense Setup

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether strong suspense is set at end of each episode
- Whether suspense is attractive
- Whether suspense effectively drives continued viewing

**Excellent Standards**:
- Must set strong suspense at end of each episode
- Suspense is engaging
- Suspense powerfully drives continued viewing

## Benchmark Analysis

### Benchmarking Market Hits

When evaluating, always benchmark against market hit series:

- **Strength Analysis**: Compared to hits, what are this story's strengths
- **Weakness Analysis**: Compared to hits, what are this story's weaknesses
- **Differentiation**: How to differentiate from similar series

## Optimization Suggestions

### Principles of Optimization Suggestions

1. **Specific and Actionable**: Suggestions should be specific and executable
2. **Targeted**: Provide specific improvement plans for specific problems
3. **Quantifiable Metrics**: Provide measurable improvement goals as much as possible

### Common Optimization Directions

**Core Satisfaction Point Optimization**:
- Enhance universality of satisfaction points
- Make satisfaction points more concrete and visualizable
- Design layered progression rhythm of satisfaction points

**Story Type Optimization**:
- Add strong setup elements
- Integrate innovation into classic patterns
- Push tropes to the extreme

**Character Setting Optimization**:
- Enhance protagonist's contrast sense
- Make protagonist desire more clear
- Make antagonists more caricatured but effective

**Character Relationship Optimization**:
- Enhance binary opposition of relationships
- Make relationships more tightly bonded
- Design clear power structure

**Plot Bridge Optimization**:
- Skip process, cut directly to conflict
- Make emotions more extreme
- Enhance visual transformation effect
- Set strong suspense hooks

## Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Deep Reading

- Fully read and understand story text
- Grasp overall structure and core elements

### Step 2: Dimension Analysis

**Core Satisfaction Point Analysis**:
- Summarize core satisfaction points
- Analyze universality degree
- Evaluate clarity and concreteness
- Check rhythmic progression

**Story Type Analysis**:
- Summarize story type
- Evaluate strong setup
- Check classic + innovation
- Determine extremization degree

**Character Setting Analysis**:
- Outline main character personas
- Evaluate contrast sense
- Check desire clarity
- Analyze functionalization degree

**Character Relationship Analysis**:
- Outline main character relationships
- Evaluate strong conflict degree
- Check strong bonding degree
- Analyze power structure

**Plot Bridge Analysis**:
- Outline plot bridges
- Evaluate conflict entry method
- Check emotional extremization degree
- Analyze visual transformation effect
- Check suspense setup

### Step 3: Comprehensive Evaluation

- Benchmark against market hits for analysis
- Form overall evaluation
- Give recommendations on whether to advance project
- Provide specific actionable optimization suggestions

## Hit Case Analysis

### Hit Satisfaction Point Types

1. **Identity Reversal Type**: Hidden identity revealed, slap antagonists
2. **Counterattack Revenge Type**: Underdog counterattacks, successful retaliation
3. **Sweet Romance Type**: Domineering CEO's extreme doting
4. **Rebirth Transmigration Type**: Rebirth changes destiny, transmigration with cheats
5. **Suspense Mystery Type**: Crack mysteries, truth revealed

### Hit Trope Patterns

- **Extremization**: Push classic tropes to the extreme
- **Fast Pace**: Tight rhythm, no dragging
- **Strong Conflict**: Strong conflict, full of tension
- **Satisfaction Release**: Satisfaction release points in every episode
