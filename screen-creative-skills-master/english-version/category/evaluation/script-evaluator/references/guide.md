# Film and TV Script Evaluation Guide

## Evaluation Core Philosophy

Film and TV script evaluation conducts professional and rigorous analysis and scoring of scripts from three dimensions: ideological nature, artistic quality, and watchability, providing reference basis for script development decisions.

---

## Evaluation Framework

### I. Ideological Nature Evaluation

Ideological nature evaluation judges whether the script's ideological nature is "positive," "neutral," or "negative."

#### 1. Values Evaluation

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether script content has correct value orientation
- Whether it transmits positive energy
- Whether it conforms to mainstream social values

**Qualitative Standards**:
- **Positive**: Transmits positive values, promotes truth, goodness and beauty
- **Neutral**: Values are neutral, unbiased
- **Negative**: Values have deviations or problems

#### 2. Social Significance Evaluation

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether it has social significance reflecting reality
- Whether it showcases contemporary Chinese people's spiritual outlook
- Whether it reflects era characteristics and social problems

**Qualitative Standards**:
- **Positive**: Has profound practical significance and positive guidance
- **Neutral**: Social significance is average or not obvious
- **Negative**: Social significance is negative or missing

---

### II. Artistic Quality Evaluation

Artistic quality evaluation judges whether the script's artistic quality is "excellent," "acceptable," or "lacking."

#### 1. Detail Portrayal

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether details are authentic and credible
- Whether details serve character shaping and plot advancement
- Whether details have layered sense

**Excellent Standards**:
- Details are rich and authentic
- Details closely related to characters and plot
- Details progress in layers, have depth

#### 2. Creative Presentation

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether script has unique creative points
- Whether creativity is novel and attractive
- Whether creativity runs throughout

**Excellent Standards**:
- Unique creativity, eye-catching
- Creativity perfectly integrated with story
- Creativity continuously attracts audience

#### 3. Narrative Logic

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether story logic is rigorous
- Whether plot development is reasonable
- Whether causal relationships are clear

**Excellent Standards**:
- Rigorous logic, no obvious logical loopholes
- Natural and smooth plot development
- Clear and reasonable causal relationships

#### 4. Narrative Techniques

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether effective narrative techniques are employed
- Whether techniques serve story expression
- Whether technique application is appropriate

**Excellent Standards**:
- Skilled narrative technique application
- Perfect combination of techniques and content
- Techniques enhance story expression power

#### 5. Narrative Rhythm

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether rhythm has tension and relaxation
- Whether rhythm serves plot development
- Whether rhythm changes are natural

**Excellent Standards**:
- Rhythm has tension and relaxation, neither dragging nor abrupt
- Rhythm matches plot development
- Natural and smooth rhythm changes

#### 6. Dialogue Expression

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether dialogue fits character personality
- Whether dialogue drives plot development
- Whether dialogue has expressive power

**Excellent Standards**:
- Vivid dialogue, fits character personality
- Dialogue drives plot, not hollow
- Dialogue has appeal and expressive power

---

### III. Watchability Evaluation

Watchability evaluation combines scoring standards to score the overall watchability of script content.

#### 1. Audience Foundation

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether content matches target audience
- Whether it can attract target audience
- Whether it satisfies audience expectations

**Excellent Standards**:
- Precisely position target audience
- Content highly fits audience needs
- Exceed audience expectations

#### 2. Topic Nature

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether theme has topic nature
- Whether it can trigger discussion
- Whether it matches social hotspots

**Excellent Standards**:
- Strong topic nature, attention-grabbing
- Has potential to trigger discussion
- Closely follows social hotspots or public concerns

#### 3. Genre Style

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether genre has appeal
- Whether style is distinct and unique
- Whether genre and style are unified

**Excellent Standards**:
- Strong genre appeal
- Distinct and unique style
- Perfect unity of genre and style

#### 4. Character Shaping

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether characters are three-dimensional and full
- Whether character personalities are distinct
- Whether character arcs are complete

**Excellent Standards**:
- Three-dimensional full characters, flesh and blood
- Distinct and unique character personalities
- Complete and clear character arcs

#### 5. Character Relationships

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether character relationships are complex and interesting
- Whether relationships drive plot development
- Whether relationships have dramatic tension

**Excellent Standards**:
- Complex and interesting character relationships
- Relationships effectively drive plot
- Relationships full of dramatic tension

#### 6. Plot Bridges

**Evaluation Points**:
- Whether plot has dramatic tension
- Whether bridge design is clever
- Whether it attracts continued viewing

**Excellent Standards**:
- Plot full of dramatic tension
- Clever and unexpected bridge design
- Strongly attracts audience to continue watching

## Scoring Standards

### Overall Scoring Standards

| Score Range | Level | Description | Development Recommendation |
|------------|-------|-------------|---------------------------|
| 8.5 and above | Excellent | Extremely strong competitiveness and development value | Directly advance subsequent development |
| 8.0-8.4 | Good | Strong competitiveness, needs minor modifications | Advance development after modifications |
| 7.5-7.9 | Qualified | Average competitiveness, needs major modifications | Evaluate after major modifications |
| 7.4 and below | Poor | Almost no competitiveness | Not recommended to advance |

### Scoring Principles

1. **Strictly Control High Scores**: Scores of 8.5 and above must be strictly controlled, only fewer scripts can achieve
2. **Ambiguous Cases Handling**: When level cannot be clearly judged, tend to give higher score
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: Scoring must combine three dimensions, cannot score based on single aspect

## Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Deep Reading

- Carefully read script content
- Understand script's themes and characters
- Grasp script's style and rhythm

### Step 2: Dimension Analysis

**Ideological Nature Analysis**:
- Analyze value orientation
- Evaluate social significance
- Give overall qualitative assessment

**Artistic Quality Analysis**:
- Evaluate six artistic dimensions
- Give scores respectively
- Summarize strengths and weaknesses

**Watchability Analysis**:
- Evaluate six watchability dimensions
- Give scores respectively
- Summarize strengths and weaknesses

### Step 3: Overall Evaluation

- Combine analysis of all three dimensions
- Form overall evaluation and total score
- Give advancement recommendations or modification suggestions

## Common Questions

### Question 1: How to judge whether values are correct?

**Answer**:
- Whether conforms to mainstream social values
- Whether transmits positive energy
- Whether respects social public order and customs

### Question 2: How to distinguish artistic quality and watchability?

**Answer**:
- **Artistic Quality**: Evaluate from professional creation perspective, focus on techniques, methods, artistic standards
- **Watchability**: Evaluate from audience reception perspective, focus on appeal, topic nature, continued viewing motivation

### Question 3: What's the significance of scoring to one decimal place?

**Answer**:
- More finely distinguish script quality
- Provide more accurate reference for decision-making
- Facilitate horizontal comparison of different scripts

---

## Evaluation Notes

1. **Objective and Fair**: Evaluate based on actual script content, not influenced by personal preferences
2. **Rigorous and Detailed**: Strictly follow evaluation framework for detailed analysis
3. **Constructive**: Provide specific and actionable modification suggestions
4. **Professional**: Use professional evaluation terminology and standards
