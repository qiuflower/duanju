# Major Plot Point Analysis Guide

## Plot Point Definition

Plot points are key events in a story that drive plot development or change character fate. They are the basic units of story structure, marking story progress and turning points.

## Plot Point Characteristics

1. **Propulsive**: Drives story forward
2. **Turning Point**: Changes story direction or character fate
3. **Conflict**: Contains conflict or contradiction
4. **Irreversible**: Once occurred, story cannot return to previous state

## Story Structure Types

### Three-Act Structure

**Act 1: Setup**
- Introduce protagonist and world view
- Establish protagonist's goals and desires
- Inciting incident occurs, protagonist embarks on journey

**Act 2: Confrontation**
- Protagonist faces obstacles and challenges
- Experiences failure and growth
- Midpoint turning point, story enters new stage

**Act 3: Resolution**
- Climactic confrontation
- Story conflicts resolved
- Protagonist achieves growth or change

### Five-Act Structure

**Act 1: Introduction**
- Introduce protagonist and background
- Establish story tone

**Act 2: Development**
- Inciting incident occurs
- Protagonist embarks on journey
- Faces initial challenges

**Act 3: Crisis**
- Protagonist encounters major setback
- Enters low point
- Must make choice

**Act 4: Climax**
- Protagonist makes final choice
- Faces greatest challenge
- Story reaches climax

**Act 5: Resolution**
- Story conflicts resolved
- Protagonist achieves growth
- Establish new balance

### Vertical Screen Drama Structure

**Golden Three Seconds Rule**
- Must grab audience in first 3 seconds
- Cut directly to conflict
- Set strong suspense

**Expectation-Suppression-Explosion Structure**
- **Establish Expectation**: Quickly establish audience expectations
- **Accumulate Suppression**: Accumulate emotions through various obstacles
- **Explosion Release**: Emotions得到极致释放

## Plot Point Extraction Techniques

### Technique 1: Identify Turning Points

**What are turning points?**
- Moments when protagonist's fate changes
- Moments when story direction changes
- Moments when character relationships change

**How to identify?**
1. Look for "but," "however" and other transition words
2. Look for major decision moments
3. Look for unexpected event occurrences

### Technique 2: Identify Conflict Points

**What are conflict points?**
- Protagonist vs. antagonist opposition
- Protagonist internal conflict
- Protagonist vs. environment confrontation

**How to identify?**
1. Look for quarrel, confrontation, competition scenes
2. Look for protagonist's predicaments and challenges
3. Look for protagonist's choices and decisions

### Technique 3: Identify Emotional Points

**What are emotional points?**
- Moments triggering strong emotions
- Moments of major emotional change
- Moments when audience resonates

**How to identify?**
1. Look for love, family, friendship related scenes
2. Look for success, failure, setback related scenes
3. Look for sacrifice, redemption, growth related scenes

### Technique 4: Identify Climax Points

**What are climax points?**
- Story's most tense moments
- Most intense confrontations
- Key moments when fate is ultimately determined

**How to identify?**
1. Look for final confrontation or decisive battle
2. Look for truth revelation moments
3. Look for key moments of fate turning

## Stage Division Methods

### Method 1: Chronological Order

Divide stages according to story development chronological order:
- Early: Protagonist's starting point
- Middle: Protagonist's growth
- Late: Protagonist achieves goals

### Method 2: Plot Development

Divide stages according to plot development logic:
- Establishment: Settings and background
- Development: Conflicts and challenges
- Climax: Final confrontation
- Resolution: Conflicts resolved

### Method 3: Theme Deepening

Divide stages according to theme deepening degree:
- Surface: Surface conflicts
- Deep: Internal contradictions
- Core: Theme sublimation

## Plot Point Expression Standards

### Standard 1: Concise and Clear

- Each plot point no more than 150 words
- Use refined language to summarize core content
- Avoid lengthy descriptions and explanations

### Standard 2: Accurate and Complete

- Accurately reflect story original meaning
- Include key information and core elements
- Don't omit important plots

### Standard 3: Logically Coherent

- Logical relationships between plot points
- Arrange according to time or cause-effect relationships
- Form complete story context

### Standard 4: Unified Format

- Use unified expression format
- Don't use Arabic numerals for numbering
- Use [Stage X] as stage title

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-Generalization

**Problem**: Plot points too general, lacking specific information

**Example**:
- Wrong: Protagonist experiences some difficulties
- Correct: Protagonist publicly questions supervisor's wrong decision at company meeting

### Mistake 2: Omitting Key Points

**Problem**: Missing important plot turning points

**Example**:
- Wrong: Protagonist and antagonist final confrontation
- Correct: Protagonist exposes antagonist's conspiracy, confronts in public

### Mistake 3: Confused Order

**Problem**: Plot point order doesn't match story development

**Solution**: Strictly arrange according to story development chronological order

### Mistake 4: Subjective Interpretation

**Problem**: Add subjective evaluation or creation

**Solution**: Strictly summarize according to story original text, do not create

## Best Practices

### Practice 1: Read Full Text

Before extracting plot points, first read complete story, grasp overall structure.

### Practice 2: Mark Key Events

While reading, mark key events and turning points, convenient for subsequent organization.

### Practice 3: Multiple Revisions

After first draft completion, revise and polish multiple times, ensure plot point expression accurate and complete.

### Practice 4: Check Against Original

After extraction completion, check against original text, ensure no omissions and errors.

## Practical Case Analysis

### Case: "Palace Intrigue Rise to Power"

**Story Synopsis**:
Su Wan enters palace and gains favor, framed by Empress and sent to Cold Palace. In Cold Palace meets deposed Tai Fei, learns palace struggle techniques. Finally Su Wan exposes Empress's conspiracy, becomes Empress.

**Plot Point Extraction**:

[Stage 1: Entering Palace and Gaining Favor]
- Su Wan participates in selection, chosen by Emperor
- Through wisdom and caution, stands firm in harem
- Gains Emperor's favor, attracts Empress's attention

[Stage 2: Being Framed and Beaten]
- Empress jealous of Su Wan's favor
- Designs framing Su Wan to lose composure before Emperor
- Su Wan sent to Cold Palace

[Stage 3: Cold Palace Growth]
- Meets deposed Tai Fei
- Learns palace struggle techniques
- From innocent girl to unfathomable

[Stage 4: Successful Rise to Power]
- Risks life to save Emperor, regains favor
- Secretly collects Empress's criminal evidence
- Publicly exposes Empress's conspiracy
- Becomes new Empress

**Analysis**:
- Divide 4 stages by chronological order
- Each stage contains 2-3 plot points
- Plot points logically coherent, form complete story context
- Covers major plot turning points of story
